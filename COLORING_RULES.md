
# Tron Capture - Grid Dominion Rules

The goal of the game is to claim more of the energy grid than your opponent. You achieve this by creating closed energy loops with your vessel's trail.

## How to Capture Sectors

1.  **Pilot Your Vessel**: On your turn, move your ship from one node to another, leaving a brilliant trail of light behind you. You have a limited number of moves per turn.
2.  **Form a Loop**: To capture a sector of the grid, you must create a closed loop. A loop can be formed in two main ways:
    *   **Path Loop**: Your active trail connects back to an earlier point on the same trail.
    *   **Territory Loop**: Your active trail connects to any part of your own previously claimed (colored) territory.
3.  **Claiming Territory**: When a loop is closed, a spectacular event occurs:
    *   The boundary of the loop becomes permanently yours, glowing intensely in your color.
    *   The space within the loop crystallizes into a black mirror and instantly shatters, revealing the fully claimed 3D shape (a collection of tetrahedra, or "pyramids") underneath. This becomes your captured sector.

## Scoring

You score points based on the geometry you capture:
-   **Pyramids**: The most valuable. These are the solid 3D volumes you create by enclosing space.
-   **Triangles**: Closed 2D faces that are not part of a larger pyramid.
-   **Lines**: Individual edges that are not part of a larger shape.
-   **Bonus Moves**: Any moves you have left over at the end of a successful turn are converted into bonus points.

## Strategic Notes

*   **Cutting Off Opponents**: You can strategically close a loop across your opponent's *active trail*. This will destabilize their trail, and any part no longer connected to their ship will be erased.
*   **Building on Territory**: Use your existing territory as a stable base to form new loops more efficiently.
*   **Undo**: If you make a mistake on your trail, press `Backspace` to undo the last step.
*   **Sudden Death**: After 10 rounds, the playable grid begins to collapse. Plan your moves carefully as space becomes limited!
